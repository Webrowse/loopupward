# Capture: emptying your head

Capture is the front door of the app, and the only part you must use *fast*. A thought lives here as a **seed**: raw text, no category, no decision made.

## Capturing

Type the thought, press enter, done. Capture on the go — waiting for coffee, mid-walk, the second the idea appears. The bar for what "deserves" capturing should be on the floor: if it occupied your mind, it qualifies.

Good seeds look like:

> "gift for Ravi — something with cycling?"
> "why am I always tired at 4pm"
> "learn enough Spanish for the Mexico trip"
> "cancel that subscription"

## What happens to a seed

Seeds wait in the inbox until you decide, and a seed only ever moves when *you* move it:

- **Organize it** — turn it into a real thing: a goal, habit, note, task for a specific day. The seed archives itself and the new thing lands in its section (the app tells you where, with a link). There's an undo right after, if you regret the shape you chose.
- **Later** — not ready to decide? Park it. It moves out of the inbox but stays reachable.
- **Archive** — the thought mattered enough to write but needs nothing. Kept, hidden.
- **Delete** — gone, but only ever with a confirmation. Personal thoughts never vanish silently.

## The inbox habit

The inbox works best **emptied regularly, not instantly**. Capture all day without deciding anything; then once a day or every few days, sit down calm and sort. Deciding is a different mental mode than capturing — the app deliberately doesn't ask you to do both at once.

## Examples

| You captured | A good shape for it |
|---|---|
| "gift for Ravi — cycling?" | One-time task on a date a week before the birthday |
| "why am I always tired at 4pm" | A note — it needs thinking, not scheduling |
| "learn Spanish for the Mexico trip" | A goal in your Travel/Growth area, horizon "This year" |
| "cancel that subscription" | Task on Today. Two minutes. Just do it. |
| "in bed by 11" | A habit, repeats every day |
