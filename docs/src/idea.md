# The idea behind LoopUpward

## Thoughts disappear. That's the whole problem.

If your brain produces more thoughts than you can act on — career worries while brushing your teeth, a gift idea in traffic, "I should really exercise" while climbing stairs — you know the two failure modes:

1. **You forget.** The good idea from Tuesday is simply gone.
2. **You don't forget.** Everything stays in your head at once, nothing has an order, and choosing what to do first becomes so heavy that you do nothing. Overthinking paralysis.

LoopUpward is built on a simple deal with your brain: *write it down the moment it appears, and you're allowed to stop thinking about it.* The thought isn't lost — it's waiting, and there's a path that takes it from "waiting" to "done."

## The loop

Everything in the app is one repeating loop:

**Capture → Organize → Act → Reflect**

- **Capture** — get the thought out of your head in five seconds. No categories, no decisions.
- **Organize** — later, calmly, decide what it is: a goal? a habit? just a note? File it where it belongs.
- **Act** — every day, Plan shows a short list. You work the list, not your anxiety.
- **Reflect** — the journal and reviews turn your history into proof that the loop works.

You don't have to use all of it. Capturing alone already helps. But each part feeds the next, and the further a thought travels around the loop, the less it weighs.

## One consistent rule set

The app tries hard to behave the same way everywhere, so you learn it once:

- **Everything you create is a "thing" (an item).** A goal, a habit, a note, a book — same object, different clothing. Any thing can live inside any other thing, move between areas, and change its shape later. Nothing is locked in.
- **Progress flows upward.** Finish a chapter and the book moves; the book moves and the yearly goal moves. You never update a percentage by hand unless you want to.
- **Checking a box means the same thing wherever you see it.** One rule, explained in [The completion rule](./completion.md).
- **Nothing vanishes silently.** Completed items stay visible (struck through), deleted items go to a recoverable Trash, unfinished tasks carry forward without shame.
