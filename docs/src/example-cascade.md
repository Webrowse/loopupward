# One goal, top to bottom — a worked example

The promise: *if I do my small list today, I am provably moving toward my year goal.* Here is the full setup, once, so the shape is clear. It takes about three minutes.

## The ambition

> "I want to read 5 books this year."

## Build the ladder

**1. The year goal.** In Life, add a goal to your Growth area:

- *Read 5 books in 2026* — kind **Goal**, tracker **Counter**, target **5**, unit "books", planning horizon **This year**.

**2. The current book.** Add a thing *inside* it:

- *Finish "Linchpin"* — kind **Book**, tracker chapter **0 / 20**, horizon **This month**.

**3. This week's slice.** You want 7 chapters this week. Open the book, *break off a piece for today*:

- *"Read chapter 1"* — moves the meter by **1**.

That's the whole setup. No week-goal object is even required — the book's meter *is* the week's scoreboard. (If you like an explicit weekly target, nest a counter "7 chapters this week", horizon **This week**, inside the book. The horizon stepper knows a week inside a month stays inside that month.)

## Live a day of it

Tomorrow morning, Plan shows *Read chapter 1* with a "+1 chapters" tag. You read, you check it:

- the task completes,
- *Linchpin* becomes chapter 1/20 — visible in Life, in the month list, everywhere,
- *Read 5 books in 2026* nudges forward, because progress flows upward through the nesting.

Each day, break off the next chapter — or set a **repeat** on the book ("weekdays") so a reading row appears by itself and you log chapters as you go.

## Finish a book

When the meter hits 20/20 the book completes itself. Bump the year counter: 1 of 5 books. Add the next book inside the year goal and the ladder keeps going.

## When to trace, and when not to

Not everything deserves this. "Give clothes for washing" is a task on today, full stop. The ladder is for the three or four ambitions where you genuinely want the answer to *"is today connected to anything?"* to be yes — and visible.
