# Reflect: journal and reviews

Reflect is where the loop closes: the app takes the days you actually lived and shows them back to you.

## The daily journal

Lives right on the Today screen (and under Reflect):

- **"Today I'm thinking about…"** — a morning brain-dump box. Unstructured on purpose.
- **End of day** — what went well, what to try differently. Two sentences beat zero.
- **Mood and energy** — a 1–5 tap each. Thirty days of these dots teach you more about your 4pm slump than any productivity article.

One entry per day; everything is editable later. Journal pages can be exported.

## Reviews

A review assembles a period — **week, month** (and **quarter, year** with Premium) — from your real history:

- what you completed, and which life areas it served,
- habit consistency (days showing up, streaks),
- meters that moved: pages, rupees, kilometers, chapters,
- your mood/energy pattern,
- a free-writing box for the review itself.

Navigate periods like a calendar — last week, March, Q2. From any week/month list in Plan, *"Reflect on this week →"* jumps to the matching review.

## Unfinished ≠ failure

Reviews deliberately don't scold. An unfinished plan is **information**: the week was overloaded, or the goal is mis-sized, or that habit needs a different time of day. The point of reflecting is to adjust the next loop, not to grade the last one.

## A five-minute Sunday

1. Open Reflect → this week.
2. Read what happened (it's already assembled).
3. Write three lines: proudest thing, hardest thing, one change for next week.
4. Open Plan → Week tab → next week, and place that one change in it.

That's a complete personal review system, five minutes, every week.

*(Free accounts see roughly the last 12 weeks of history; Premium keeps all of it, plus quarterly and yearly reviews.)*
