# Routines: the parts of the day that run on rails

A routine is a timetable. You write it once — an ordered list of steps, each with the minutes it takes, if you want them — and from then on it just shows up. A morning routine, a night routine, gym, study: sequences you do the same way every time, that shouldn't cost you a single planning thought again.

## What a routine is made of

- **A name** — "Morning", "Night", "Gym", anything.
- **A script** — ordered steps: *wash face (5), meditation (15), brush teeth (5)*. The order matters; drag steps up and down by their handle. Minutes are optional per step — "take meds" has no duration, and that's fine.
- **Visible hours** — from when to when it sits on your Today list. A morning routine set to 5:00 am – 12:00 pm disappears after noon; a night routine set to 9:00 pm – 2:00 am shows up in the evening (an end before the start simply wraps past midnight). Outside its hours it waits under a quiet "Out of hours" line — never a nagging task. No hours set means it shows all day.
- **A schedule** — routines repeat daily unless you say otherwise, and they're checked off per day with a streak, exactly like habits.

The minutes add up (5 + 15 + 5 = **25 min**) and Today shows the routine wearing its length: **"3 steps · 25 min"**.

## One line on Today, always

Steps never appear in your todo list. The whole routine is **one entry** with a round checkbox; the steps live inside it. Your day list stays short even if your morning script has nine steps.

There are three ways to work through one:

1. **Tick the round checkbox** on Today — logs the whole day at once, no questions.
2. **Tick steps one by one** on the **Routines** page (the 🌄 Routines link above the day's list). Ticking the last step logs the day by itself — and un-ticking a step takes the log back. Mid-way through, the Today row shows "1/3 steps".
3. **Run it with the step timer** — the good one.

## The step runner

Open the routine's focus timer (the clock icon on its row, or ▶ Run on the Routines page) and press **Start the routine**. It walks the script in order:

- Each timed step gets its own countdown. Finishing a step rolls **straight into the next one's timer** — no fiddling between steps.
- A step with no minutes saved asks once: give it a length, or choose *"no timer, just show it"* — the step stays on screen (with a quiet count-up) until you mark it done. Meds don't take five minutes; they take until you've taken them.
- **Skip for now** sends the current step to the back of the line instead of pretending it happened. It comes around again at the end.
- Progress is saved per step, per day — closing the runner mid-way loses nothing, and the ticks are already on every other device.
- The last step's check logs the routine's day and feeds the streak.

## Where routines live

The **Routines** page (linked from Today) lists them all — each with its hours, steps, minutes and streak, tickable on the spot. New ones start from a template (morning, night) or from scratch: name it, give it hours, then write its script on its page. A routine is a normal life node too — it can live in an area, and "Retire routine" ends it with its history intact.
