# Getting started in ten minutes

A first session that touches every part of the app once.

## 1. Capture three thoughts (1 minute)

Open **Capture** and type whatever is on your mind right now, one thought per entry:

> "need to fix my sleep"
> "mom's birthday — plan something"
> "that book about deep work everyone mentions"

That's it. They're safe. Notice how your head feels slightly quieter.

## 2. Make a life area (1 minute)

Open **Life** → **+ Area**. Areas are the big departments of your life — most people want 3 or 4 to start:

- 💪 Health
- 💼 Career
- ❤️ Family
- 💰 Money

(The free plan includes 4 areas, which is plenty to begin.)

## 3. Give one thought a shape (2 minutes)

Back in **Capture**, take "need to fix my sleep" and organize it. Choose what it is — say, a **habit**: "In bed by 11pm", repeating **every day**, in your **Health** area.

The moment it has a repeat, it will show up on your day list automatically. You never have to remember it again.

## 4. Plan today (2 minutes)

Open **Plan**. Your new habit is already there. Add one or two one-time tasks with *"Add something for today…"*:

> "Call about mom's birthday" ⭐ Important

That's a complete day plan. Two or three things. The morning question — *"what should I do today?"* — now has a written answer.

## 5. Do one, check one (1 minute)

Do the easiest thing on the list, then tap its checkbox. The progress bar moves. If the task belonged to a goal, the goal moved too — everywhere at once.

## 6. Close the day (2 minutes)

In the evening, Plan has a small journal on the same screen: how the day went, mood, energy. Two sentences is enough. On Sunday, open **Reflect** and it will show you the week you actually had.
