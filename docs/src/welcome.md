# Welcome

LoopUpward is a place to put the things your brain keeps generating — ideas, worries, goals, plans, half-thoughts on a staircase — so they stop circling in your head and start lining up into days you can actually live.

This guide explains **every part of the app from your side of the screen**: what each section is for, what every button does, and how the pieces connect. Each chapter ends with concrete examples.

## The app in one paragraph

You **capture** a thought the moment it appears (before it evaporates). When a thought keeps coming back, you give it a shape in **Life** — a goal, a habit, a book you're reading — and file it under the part of life it belongs to. **Plan** turns those shapes into concrete days: a short list every morning of what would make today a win. **Notes** hold the longer thoughts that don't need doing, and **Reflect** shows you, with your own real history, that you're moving.

## The six sections

| Section | What it holds | When you open it |
|---|---|---|
| **Capture** | Raw, unsorted thoughts ("seeds") | The second an idea strikes |
| **Plan** | Today's list, plus this week / month / quarter / year | Every morning, and when planning ahead |
| **Life** | Your life areas and everything you're growing in them | When organizing or checking a goal |
| **Notes** | Longer writing that isn't a task — markdown, in folders | When a thought needs paragraphs |
| **Reflect** | Daily journal, weekly and monthly reviews | End of the day, end of the week |
| **Settings** | Account, sync, appearance, Simple mode, your data | Rarely |
